When music moves: Choreography in Film

This page highlights the powerful synergy between music and dance within the music video format. We've curated standout examples where choreography transcends mere accompaniment, becoming a vital component of the visual narrative. Each demonstrates how movement can effectively convey emotion, enhance artistic expression, and significantly elevate the overall viewing experience.

Radiohead - Lotus Flower

The music video for Radiohead's 'Lotus Flower' is a captivating example of how choreography can be both minimal and deeply expressive. Directed by Garth Jennings, the video features Thom Yorke in a solo performance, showcasing a unique and idiosyncratic dance. The choreography, if it can be called that, is characterized by Yorke's fluid, almost spasmodic movements, which perfectly complement the song's rhythmic and hypnotic nature. There's a sense of raw, uninhibited expression in his movements, conveying a mix of vulnerability and intensity. The video's stark, black-and-white aesthetic further emphasizes the performance, allowing Yorke's movements to take center stage. The 'dance' in 'Lotus Flower' isn't about technical precision; it's about conveying emotion and connecting with the music on a visceral level.

Massive Attack, Tricky & 3D - Take It There

The music video for Massive Attack's 'Take It There' is a striking example of how choreography can be used to create a sense of unease and tension. Directed by Hiro Murai, the video features a series of unsettling dance sequences performed by a group of young dancers. The choreography is characterized by its jerky, spasmodic movements, which create a sense of disjointedness and alienation. The dancers' expressions are blank and emotionless, adding to the video's overall sense of dread. The choreography in 'Take It There' is not meant to be beautiful or graceful; rather, it is meant to be disturbing and thought-provoking.

Alt-J - The Actor

The music video for Alt-J's 'The Actor' presents a captivating narrative woven with expressive choreography. Directed by Saskia Dixie, the video features intricate and emotive movements that mirror the song's themes of transformation and performance. The choreography is characterized by its blend of contemporary dance and theatrical gestures, creating a sense of drama and intrigue. The dancers' interactions are both fluid and precise, conveying a range of emotions from vulnerability to intensity. The choreography in 'The Actor' serves to amplify the song's storytelling, adding a visual dimension that is both mesmerizing and thought-provoking.
